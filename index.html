I am a PhD student at Pompeu Fabra University (UPF) in Barcelona, under the supervision of Gemma Boleda.

My research is in the framework of Language and Vision: I investigate how humans use different referring expressions depending on the visual context, on the one side, and the intrinsic properties of the objects, on the other. Currently, I am focusing on object naming, trying to understand which factors determine different conceptualizations of an object.

My work is part of the ERC project AMORE: A distributional MOdel of Reference to Entities, led by Gemma Boleda. The aim of the project is to enable machines to connect language to reality, and understand how people make this connection. Therefore, we run computational modeling experiments to explore the phenomenon of reference in natural language, and the interaction of language with conceptual knowledge and the extralinguistic context. I am also a member of the UPF research group COLT (Computational Linguistics and Linguistic Theory).
